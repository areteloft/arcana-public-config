Indie tarot project · Early testing

Translating a personal tarot reading style into a digital experience.

This is a small indie project built around one question: can a tarot experience still feel personal, immersive, and reflective in a digital format? I wanted to bring my own way of reading into an app with a tactile card-drawing flow, a quiet atmosphere, and a distinct world of its own.

Join closed testing See screenshots
What I’m trying to build

Not just instant answers, but the feeling of drawing cards by hand, stepping into a mood, and staying with a reading long enough for it to mean something.

Direct card-drawing interaction
A distinct, immersive tarot world
Reflective reading tone over generic output

About the project

The app is built around the experience itself: writing a question, drawing cards directly, and receiving a reading inside a mood-driven world rather than a flat utility screen.

Why I started this

I wanted to see whether a reading style that feels personal on paper could be carried into a digital format without losing its tone. That meant focusing not only on interpretation, but also on atmosphere, pacing, and the act of drawing the cards itself.

What matters most

1A card-drawing UI that feels intentional, not mechanical.
2An immersive setting with its own mood and internal logic.
3Readings that feel reflective and coherent rather than generic.
